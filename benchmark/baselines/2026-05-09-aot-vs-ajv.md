## ata-AOT vs AJV-runtime

| Dimension | Schema | ata-AOT | AJV-runtime | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Bundle (gz) | S1 (simple) | 955 B | 52.7 KB | 56.5x smaller |
| Bundle (gz) | S2 (complex) | 1.6 KB | 52.7 KB | 32.0x smaller |

**Conclusion:** ata-AOT wins clearly on bundle size, cold start, compile time, throughput.

---

*Measured on Apple M4 Pro — Node.js v25.2.1 — 2026-05-09*

> **Bundle size note (AJV):** Measured in a fresh child process by tracing all
> 71 CJS modules evaluated during `new Ajv() + addFormats() + .compile(schema)`
> (250.5 KB raw, 52.7 KB gzipped).
> A tree-shaken esbuild bundle would be marginally smaller; this is the faithful
> floor for a standard `require('ajv')` setup. ata-AOT ships only the per-schema
> generated function with zero runtime library dependency.
